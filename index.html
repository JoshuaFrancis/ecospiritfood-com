The collection

Four wild pantry staples.
One Canadian landscape.

  1. 01

    Birch Syrup

    Yukon Territory

    Tapped from paper birch in early spring. Ten times rarer than maple. Smoky, mineral, faintly caramel. The syrup that serious kitchens reach for when maple is too obvious.

  2. 02

    Spruce Tip Salt

    Pacific Northwest, BC

    New growth tips harvested in the two-week window each May. Blended with hand-raked fleur de sel. Citrus-forward, resinous, unmistakably northern. Finishing salt for fish, roast vegetables, butter.

  3. 03

    Wild Mushroom Powder

    Interior BC

    Chanterelle, porcini, and pine mushroom foraged from the Interior plateau. Slow-dried and stone-milled. One teaspoon in a braise does what a stock takes four hours to build.

  4. 04

    Labrador Tea

    Northern Ontario

    Wild-harvested from boreal bogs, hand-dried in small batches. Floral, slightly medicinal, deeply Canadian. Steeped as tea or used as an aromatic in braised game and wild-caught fish.
